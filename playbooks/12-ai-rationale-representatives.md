# Contract Drafting Rule: Definition of "Representatives"

## 1. Rule Objective

Ensures the definition of "Representatives" is sufficiently broad to cover all necessary parties while maintaining a restrictive qualifier for liability management.

## 2. Standard Definition Components

- **Core Entities:** Recipient's affiliates and affiliated funds.
- **Personnel:** Officers, directors, employees, partners, and members.
- **External Advisors:** Legal counsel, consultants, accountants, and financial advisors.
- **Strategic Partners:** Insurers and potential financing sources.
- **Secondary Layer:** The respective representatives of any of the above parties.

## 3. The Qualifier (The "Knowledge Clause")

The definition is strictly limited to individuals or entities listed above who **actually receive Confidential Information from the Recipient or at the Recipient's direction**.

### Logic & Rationale

- **Operational Autonomy:** Disclosing information to Representatives should not require prior consent from the Disclosing Party.
- **Deal Uncertainty:** At the NDA stage, the Recipient lacks sufficient deal visibility to pre-identify every necessary stakeholder. A broad definition prevents future bottlenecks.
- **Administrative Efficiency:** Including all potential parties upfront removes the administrative burden of seeking constant amendments or waivers as the deal team expands.

## 4. Qualifier: Rationale - Receiving Party Representative Qualifier

### 1. Objective: Scope Limitation

Limits the legal and "non-disclosure" obligations of the Receiving Party to only those individuals and entities who have actually come into contact with the sensitive data.

### 2. Functional Drivers

- **Liability Containment:** Prevents the Receiving Party from being held contractually liable for the actions of every employee or affiliate in their global organization, many of whom may have no knowledge of the specific transaction.
- **Audit Trail Alignment:** Ensures that "Representatives" are defined by the actual flow of information, creating a verifiable nexus between the disclosure and the recipient.
- **Operational Reality:** In large institutional structures (e.g., Private Equity or Multinational Corps), "Affiliates" may number in the hundreds; the qualifier narrows the legal "net" to the active deal team.

### 3. Strategic Intent (The "Why")

- **Standard of Care:** It establishes that the Receiving Party is only responsible for the conduct of those it has affirmatively directed to receive the Confidential Information.
- **Burden of Proof:** Shifts the focus from a theoretical list of parties to a factual determination of who was actually "in the loop."
