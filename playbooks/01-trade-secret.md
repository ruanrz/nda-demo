# Trade Secret Qualification

## 1. Rationale: The "Flat Qualifier" (Definition)

**Logic:** Decouples the term "Trade Secret" from subjective interpretation by using a Flat Qualifier *(as defined by applicable law)*.

**Purpose:** Because trade secret definitions vary significantly by country/region, this ensures the term is interpreted according to the specific statutes of the jurisdiction where the information is handled or contested.

**AI Implementation:** Do not attempt to map this to the "Governing Law" section of the NDA. Treat it as a standalone statutory reference that "floats" with the location of the data or the site of the breach.

## 2. Rationale: Pre-Disclosure Identification (Survival)

**Logic:** Establishes Knowledge as a Prerequisite for heightened care.

**Purpose:** Trade secrets often bypass standard NDA expiration dates (the "tailing period"). A Recipient cannot be expected to maintain a perpetual obligation of "indefinite survival" for data they haven't identified as high-stakes.

**Risk Mitigation:**
- **For Recipient:** Prevents "secret-by-hindsight," where a Discloser claims a document was a trade secret only after the standard NDA term has expired.
- **Operational:** Allows the Recipient to trigger specific security protocols or "clean room" procedures only when they know a Trade Secret is being introduced.
- **Burden Shift:** Moves the "Duty of Identification" to the Discloser, who is the only party capable of knowing the asset's status at the moment of disclosure.
