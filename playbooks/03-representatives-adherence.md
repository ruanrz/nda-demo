# Representatives' Adherence Standard

## 1. Rule Summary

**Trigger:** Any clause requiring Representatives to "agree to be bound" by, "become party to," or "sign onto" the existing NDA.

## 2. Rationale: Legal Realism & Scope Control

**Privity of Contract:** A Recipient cannot legally compel a third party (like a global bank or an outside law firm) to "agree to be bound" to an NDA after the fact. Large firms often refuse to sign joinders or secondary agreements.

**The "Notice" Standard:** The Recipient's actual duty is to notify the Representative of the information's sensitivity and instruct them on the rules. "Directing to abide" reflects this operational reality.

**Applicability Filter:** Not every term in an NDA is relevant to a Representative (e.g., "Mutual Indemnification" or "Governing Law" clauses). Using the phrase "expressly applicable to such Representatives" ensures that the Representative is only responsible for the conduct rules (Confidentiality and Non-Use), not the entire legal framework of the Agreement.

## 3. Action

| Original Requirement | AI Polished Action |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Agreement to be Bound | Strike "agreed to be bound by the terms hereof" (or similar). |
| New Requirement | Insert: "direct them to abide by the terms of this Agreement that are expressly applicable to such Representatives." |
