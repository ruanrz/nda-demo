# General Rules: Entity Isolation & Operational Consistency

## Rules

| Rule | Trigger / Requirement | AI Action |
|------|----------------------|-----------|
| 1. Defined Word Consistency | Usage of inconsistent terms for the same concept (e.g., mixing "Evaluation Material" and "Confidential Information"). | Identify the primary defined term and standardize all references to match it throughout the document. |
| 2. Standard of Care | Any mention of "efforts" or "care" (e.g., best efforts, reasonable efforts, reasonable care). | Change to "commercially reasonable" (e.g., "commercially reasonable efforts" or "commercially reasonable care"). |
| 3. Subject/Verb Suffix | Any obligation imposed on both the Recipient and its Representatives. | Change to: "The Recipient shall and shall direct its Representatives to..." |
| 4. Mutual Assignment | Unilateral assignment clauses (e.g., "Recipient may not assign..."). | Make it mutual. Use: "Neither party may assign this Agreement without the prior written consent of the other party." (Note: Do not add "email being sufficient" here). |
| 5. Recipient Entity Isolation | Definition of "Recipient" or "You" that includes affiliates or subsidiaries. | Strike "and its affiliates or subsidiaries." The NDA must only bind the signatory entity. |
| 6. Target Entity Isolation | Definition of "Company" or "Discloser" that includes affiliates or subsidiaries. | Strike "and its affiliates or subsidiaries" from the Company definition to ensure the Recipient only owes duties to the identified party. |

## Polished Examples for AI Processing

**Example 1: Entity Isolation (Rules 5 & 6)**
- Original: "This Agreement is by and between ABC Corp, and its affiliates and subsidiaries (the 'Company'), and XYZ Fund, and its affiliates and subsidiaries (the 'Recipient')."
- Polished: "This Agreement is by and between ABC Corp (the 'Company') and XYZ Fund (the 'Recipient')."

**Example 2: Standard of Care & Direction (Rules 2 & 3)**
- Original: "You and your Representatives shall use best efforts to keep the Information secret."
- Polished: "The Recipient shall and shall direct its Representatives to use commercially reasonable efforts to keep the Information secret."

**Example 3: Mutual Assignment (Rule 4)**
- Original: "The Recipient may not assign its rights under this Agreement without the prior written consent of the Company."
- Polished: "Neither party may assign this Agreement or any of its rights or obligations hereunder without the prior written consent of the other party."

## Strategic "Why" for AI Reasoning

"These rules focus on Liability Ringfencing. By isolating the entities to the specific signatories, the AI prevents the Recipient from accidentally binding sister companies or becoming liable to unknown affiliates of the Target. Standardizing to 'commercially reasonable' and 'direct to' ensures the Recipient's performance is measured against achievable business standards rather than strict guarantees of third-party behavior."
